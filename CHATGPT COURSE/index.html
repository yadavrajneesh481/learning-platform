What is Prompt Engineering?

üìö

Definition

Prompt engineering is the art and science of crafting instructions to communicate with AI models effectively. It's like learning to speak the AI's language!

Prompt engineering matlab AI se baat karne ka sahi tarika. Jaise tum kisi se achhe se baat karoge toh woh samajh jayega, waise hi AI ko bhi clear instructions chahiye!

Why It Matters

Good prompts = Better results. It's the difference between asking a question clearly vs. confusing someone with unclear instructions.

Achhe prompts se achhe results milte hain. Jaise agar tum kisi se clearly puchoge toh woh sahi jawab dega, waise hi AI ke saath bhi hai!

Core Concepts Explained Simply

What is it?

It's about writing clear, specific instructions that tell the AI exactly what to do.

Simple Analogy üë∂

Imagine telling a friend to make tea. If you say "Make tea," they might be confused. But if you say "Boil water for 5 minutes, add tea bag, let it steep for 3 minutes, add milk," they'll do it perfectly!

Jaise tum apne dost ko chai banane ko kehte ho. Agar sirf "chai bana" bologe toh confuse ho jayega. Lekin agar step-by-step batao toh perfect banega!

Example:

‚ùå Bad: "Write something about cats"

‚úÖ Good: "Write a 100-word paragraph about why cats make good pets, using simple language for kids aged 8-10"

Key Points:

  • Be specific about what you want
  • Mention the format (paragraph, list, code, etc.)
  • Give word/length limits if needed
  • Specify the audience or tone

What is it?

Providing background information so the AI understands the situation better.

Simple Analogy üë∂

If you tell someone "I'm tired," they might suggest coffee. But if you say "I'm tired because I worked 12 hours," they'll suggest rest instead. Context changes the response!

Context matlab background information dena. Jaise agar tum kehte ho "main thak gaya" toh koi coffee suggest karega. Lekin agar kehte ho "12 ghante kaam kiya isliye thak gaya" toh rest suggest karenge. Context se response change hota hai!

Example:

‚ùå Without context: "Write an email"

‚úÖ With context: "Write a professional email to my boss requesting a day off next Friday. I've been working on a big project and need to rest."

Key Points:

  • Explain the situation/background
  • Mention who the audience is
  • Share any relevant constraints
  • Provide previous attempts if available

What is it?

Telling the AI what role or expertise it should take on.

Simple Analogy üë∂

If you ask a doctor vs. a chef about food, you'll get different answers. The doctor talks about nutrition, the chef talks about taste. Roles matter!

Role assignment matlab AI ko batana ki woh kaun hai. Jaise doctor se food ke baare mein puchoge toh nutrition batayega, chef se puchoge toh taste batayega. Role matter karta hai!

Example:

‚ùå No role: "Explain quantum physics"

‚úÖ With role: "You are a physics teacher explaining to 10-year-olds. Explain quantum physics using everyday examples."

Key Points:

  • Start with "You are a..."
  • Choose relevant expertise
  • Match the role to your need
  • Examples: teacher, doctor, chef, programmer, etc.

What is it?

Clearly defining what you're giving the AI (input) and what you want back (output).

Simple Analogy üë∂

Like a vending machine: You put money in (input) and get snacks out (output). Clear exchange!

Jaise vending machine mein paisa dalte ho (input) aur snacks milta hai (output). Clear exchange! Waise hi AI ko batao ki kya de rahe ho aur kya chahiye.

Example:

Input: A list of 5 products with prices

Output: A table showing which product is cheapest

Key Points:

  • Specify input format (text, list, table, etc.)
  • Specify output format clearly
  • Give examples of both if possible
  • Be explicit about structure

What is it?

Setting rules and limits for how the AI should respond.

Simple Analogy üë∂

Like rules in a game. Without rules, the game is chaos. With rules, everyone knows what to do!

Constraints matlab rules. Jaise game mein rules hote hain, waise hi AI ko bhi rules do. Bina rules ke chaos hoga, rules ke saath sab clear hai!

Example:

Constraints:

- Use simple language (no technical jargon)

- Maximum 100 words

- Friendly and encouraging tone

- Include 2 examples

Key Points:

  • Tone (formal, casual, friendly, etc.)
  • Format (bullet points, paragraphs, code, etc.)
  • Length (word count, number of items)
  • Style (technical, simple, creative, etc.)
  • What to avoid or include

What is it?

Using special symbols to clearly separate different parts of your prompt.

Simple Analogy üë∂

Like using folders to organize files. Instead of mixing everything, you put each thing in its own folder!

Delimiters matlab special symbols jo different parts ko separate karte hain. Jaise folders mein files organize karte ho, waise hi prompts mein delimiters use karo!

Example:

Using delimiters:

---TASK---

Summarize this text

---TEXT---

[Your text here]

---FORMAT---

3 bullet points

Key Points:

  • Use ---, ###, """, *** to separate sections
  • Makes prompts clearer and organized
  • Helps AI understand structure better
  • Reduces confusion and errors

Prompting Techniques Explained

üéØ Zero-Shot Prompting

Easy

What is it? Asking the AI to do something without giving any examples.

Analogy: Like asking someone to cook a dish without showing them a recipe first. They use their general knowledge!

Zero-shot matlab bina example ke seedha kaam karna. Jaise kisi ko bina recipe dikhaye dish banana bolo - woh apne knowledge se banayega!

Example:

Prompt: "Translate 'Hello' to Spanish"

AI: "Hola"

When to use: Simple tasks, general knowledge questions

Pros: Quick, no examples needed

Cons: May not work for complex tasks

üìö Few-Shot Prompting

Medium

What is it? Giving 2-3 examples before asking the AI to do the task.

Analogy: Like showing someone 2-3 examples of how to fold paper before asking them to fold a new one.

Few-shot matlab pehle 2-3 examples dikhao, phir AI ko kaam karne bolo. Jaise teacher pehle examples solve karta hai, phir tumse kehta hai "ab tum karo"!

Example:

Example 1: "Happy" ‚Üí Positive

Example 2: "Sad" ‚Üí Negative

Now classify: "Excited"

AI: "Positive"

When to use: Complex tasks, specific patterns needed

Pros: Better accuracy, AI learns from examples

Cons: Need to create examples

üîó Chain-of-Thought (CoT)

Hard

What is it? Asking the AI to show its thinking step-by-step.

Analogy: Like asking someone to explain their math solution step-by-step instead of just giving the answer.

Chain-of-thought matlab AI ko step-by-step sochne bolo. Jaise maths mein har step likhte ho, waise hi AI ko bhi har step batane bolo!

Example:

Prompt: "If I have 5 apples and buy 3 more, then give 2 to my friend, how many do I have? Think step-by-step."

AI: "Step 1: Start with 5 apples. Step 2: Buy 3 more = 5+3=8. Step 3: Give 2 away = 8-2=6. Answer: 6 apples"

When to use: Complex math, reasoning tasks

Pros: Better accuracy, easier to debug

Cons: Takes longer

üîÑ Self-Consistency

Hard

What is it? Asking the AI the same question multiple times and finding the most common answer.

Analogy: Like asking 5 people the same question and going with the answer most people give.

Self-consistency matlab same question 3-5 baar poocho aur jo answer sabse zyada baar aaye woh lo. Jaise 5 logo se puchoge aur jo sabse common answer ho woh sahi hoga!

Example:

Ask same question 3 times ‚Üí Get 3 answers ‚Üí Pick the most common one

When to use: When you need very accurate answers

Pros: Very accurate

Cons: Expensive, takes time

üìñ RAG (Retrieval-Augmented)

Expert

What is it? Giving the AI relevant documents/information before asking a question.

Analogy: Like giving someone a textbook before asking them a question about the topic.

RAG matlab AI ko pehle documents do, phir question poocho. Jaise exam mein open book test - book se answer dhundho!

Example:

[Provide: Wikipedia article about Python]

Prompt: "Based on the article, what is Python?"

When to use: Specific domain knowledge needed

Pros: More accurate, uses real data

Cons: Complex to set up

‚ö° ReAct (Reason + Act)

Expert

What is it? AI reasons about what to do, then takes action, then observes the result.

Analogy: Like a detective: Think about what to do ‚Üí Do it ‚Üí See what happens ‚Üí Think again.

ReAct matlab pehle socho, phir karo, phir result dekho. Jaise detective kaam karta hai - clue dhundho, action lo, result dekho!

Example:

Thought: "I need to search for current weather"

Action: "Search for weather in Delhi"

Observation: "It's 35¬∞C and sunny"

Thought: "Now I can answer the question"

When to use: Complex tasks needing tools/actions

Pros: Can use external tools

Cons: Very complex

Interview Questions & Answers

Prepare for your interviews with these common questions!

Q1: What is Prompt Engineering?

Answer: Prompt engineering is the practice of designing and optimizing instructions (prompts) to communicate effectively with AI language models. It involves crafting clear, specific, and well-structured inputs to get the desired outputs from AI systems.

Key Points:

  • It's about clarity and specificity
  • Good prompts = Better results
  • It's both an art and a science
  • It requires understanding how AI thinks

Q2: What's the difference between Zero-Shot and Few-Shot prompting?

Answer:

Zero-Shot: You ask the AI to do something without any examples. The AI uses its general knowledge.

Few-Shot: You provide 2-3 examples first, then ask the AI to do the task. The AI learns from the examples.

Comparison:

Aspect Zero-Shot Few-Shot
Examples needed No Yes (2-3)
Accuracy Lower Higher
Complexity Simple More complex
Best for Simple tasks Complex tasks

Q3: How do you reduce hallucinations in AI responses?

Answer: Hallucinations are when AI makes up false information. Here are ways to reduce them:

  • Be specific: Clear instructions reduce confusion
  • Use RAG: Provide real documents for reference
  • Ask for sources: "Cite your sources" or "Provide references"
  • Use constraints: "Only use information from the provided text"
  • Chain-of-thought: Ask AI to show its reasoning
  • Validation: Ask follow-up questions to verify answers

Q4: What is Chain-of-Thought prompting and why is it useful?

Answer: Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting asks the AI to show its thinking process step-by-step before giving the final answer.

Why it's useful:

  • Better accuracy for complex problems
  • Easier to debug if something goes wrong
  • Shows the reasoning process
  • Helps catch logical errors

Example:

Instead of: "What's 15 √ó 8?"

Use: "What's 15 √ó 8? Show your work step-by-step."

Q5: What are the main components of a good prompt?

Answer: A good prompt should include:

  • Role: "You are a..." - What expertise should the AI have?
  • Context: Background information about the situation
  • Task: What exactly do you want the AI to do?
  • Format: How should the answer be structured?
  • Constraints: Any rules or limitations (tone, length, etc.)
  • Examples: If possible, show what good looks like

Q6: What is RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)?

Answer: RAG is a technique where you provide relevant documents or information to the AI before asking a question.

How it works:

  1. You provide relevant documents/text
  2. AI reads and understands them
  3. You ask your question
  4. AI answers based on the provided information

Benefits:

  • More accurate answers
  • Uses real, verified data
  • Reduces hallucinations
  • Great for domain-specific questions

Q7: How do you optimize prompts for better results?

Answer: Here are key optimization strategies:

  • Be specific: Avoid vague words like "good" or "better"
  • Use examples: Few-shot prompting improves accuracy
  • Break it down: Split complex tasks into smaller steps
  • Test and refine: Try different versions and see what works
  • Use constraints: Specify tone, format, length
  • Add context: More background = better understanding
  • Iterative improvement: Learn from each attempt

Q8: What are common mistakes in prompt engineering?

Answer: Common mistakes to avoid:

  • Being too vague: "Write something good" is too unclear
  • No context: AI doesn't understand the situation
  • Unclear format: AI doesn't know how to structure the answer
  • Too many constraints: Can confuse the AI
  • No examples: For complex tasks, examples help a lot
  • Expecting perfection: Sometimes you need to refine
  • Not testing: Always verify the output

Q9: Explain the importance of temperature in prompts

Answer: Temperature controls how creative or random the AI's responses are.

Low temperature (0-0.3):

  • More focused and deterministic
  • Best for factual tasks, code generation
  • Consistent outputs

High temperature (0.7-1.0):

  • More creative and diverse
  • Best for creative writing, brainstorming
  • Varied outputs

Q10: What is the difference between system, user, and assistant messages?

Answer:

System Message: Sets the AI's behavior and personality. This persists across the conversation.

Example: "You are a helpful coding tutor who explains concepts simply."

User Message: The actual query or request from the user.

Example: "How do I create a for loop in Python?"

Assistant Message: Previous responses from the AI, used for context in multi-turn conversations.

Example: "A for loop in Python is used to iterate..."

Important Definitions

Key terms you need to know

üéØ Prompt

Definition:

A prompt is the input text or instruction you give to an AI model to generate a response.

Prompt matlab woh instruction jo tum AI ko dete ho kuch karne ke liye. Jaise tum kisi ko kehte ho "chai bana do" - yeh ek prompt hai!

ü§ñ LLM (Large Language Model)

Definition:

A Large Language Model is an AI system trained on massive amounts of text data to understand and generate human-like text.

LLM matlab bahut bada AI model jo bahut saare text pe trained hai. Jaise GPT, Claude, Gemini - yeh sab LLMs hain!

üé® Temperature

Definition:

Temperature is a parameter that controls the randomness and creativity of AI responses. Lower = more focused, Higher = more creative.

Temperature control karta hai ki AI kitna creative hoga. Kam temperature = serious answers, zyada temperature = creative answers!

üî¢ Tokens

Definition:

Tokens are pieces of text that the AI processes. Roughly, 1 token ‚âà 4 characters or 0.75 words in English.

Tokens matlab text ke chote pieces. AI tokens mein sochta hai. Jaise "Hello World" approximately 2 tokens hai!

üé≠ Hallucination

Definition:

When an AI generates false or made-up information that sounds plausible but isn't true.

Hallucination matlab jab AI jhooth bol raha hai ya galat information de raha hai jo sach lagti hai lekin hai nahi!

üìö Context Window

Definition:

The maximum amount of text (in tokens) that an AI can process at once, including both input and output.

Context window matlab kitna text AI ek baar mein process kar sakta hai. Jaise kuch models 4K tokens, kuch 128K tokens handle kar sakte hain!

üîÑ Fine-tuning

Definition:

The process of further training a pre-trained model on specific data to specialize it for particular tasks.

Fine-tuning matlab ek trained model ko aur specific kaam ke liye train karna. Jaise ek doctor ko heart specialist banana!

üìñ Embeddings

Definition:

Numerical representations of text that capture semantic meaning, allowing AI to understand relationships between words.

Embeddings matlab text ko numbers mein convert karna taaki AI samajh sake. Jaise "king" aur "queen" ke numbers similar honge!

üéØ Zero-Shot Learning

Definition:

The ability of an AI to perform tasks it wasn't explicitly trained on, without any examples.

Zero-shot matlab AI bina training ke naye kaam kar sakta hai. Jaise tum kabhi cycle nahi chalayi lekin scooter chala sakte ho!

üìä Few-Shot Learning

Definition:

Learning to perform a task from just a few examples provided in the prompt.

Few-shot matlab sirf 2-3 examples se seekhna. Jaise teacher ne 2 examples solve kiye aur tum samajh gaye!

üîó Chain-of-Thought

Definition:

A prompting technique where the AI shows its reasoning process step-by-step.

Chain-of-thought matlab step-by-step sochna. Jaise maths mein har step likhte ho, waise hi AI bhi karta hai!

üìñ RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)

Definition:

A technique that combines information retrieval with text generation by providing relevant documents to the AI.

RAG matlab AI ko documents dena aur phir question poochna. Jaise open book exam - book se answer dhundho!
